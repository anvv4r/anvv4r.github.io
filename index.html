ABOUT ME

As a full-stack web developer, I possess a strong familiarity with web architecture, various web platforms, and content management systems (CMS). My journey in IT commenced with a role as a Web Server and Content Manager at Eurolinkmedia International, a Jakarta-based start-up. Since departing in 2010, I have thrived as a web developer on a freelance basis. I am passionate about my job and my personal contribution to the world of modern web design. I like nothing more than telling stories with the help of photography, that celebrate simplicity and creativity. Currently, my interests have shifted towards Data Science, prompting me to engage in both online and offline related courses.

In addition to my other experiences, I was involved in the establishment, launch, and managing of two Cyber Cafes, known as emiNET Cafe - internet and coffee house, from 2007 to 2009. Subsequently, I ventured into the food industry and created a Chinese Food Outlet named HOTPLATE Inc., with the tagline "the hotter the better". This outlet was a part of the Kemang Food Fest in Jakarta, and it operated from 2008 to 2014.

Another area where I gained significant experience was in journalism. My journey began in 1997 when I joined Suara Mahasiswa UI. This organization published an annual student magazine and a daily bulletin named "bergerak!" during the 1998 era of Indonesian political history. In 1999, I interned at Dana Pensiun Bakrie and contributed as a writer for their annual magazine. From 2003 to 2007, I was involved in the publication of several magazines, including Figur Publik Magazine, Depok Metro Magazine, and TaSYA Kids Magazine. My journalistic journey culminated in a teaching role at the State University of Jakarta, where I taught News Writing from 2007 to 2009 as a non-permanent lecturer.
